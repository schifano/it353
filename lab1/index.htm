SUMMARIES

Google agitates for a revolution in Web development.

Google has been encouraging the adoption of a new World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) standard called web components. Web components allow for greater extensibility similar to Java Enterprise and Microsoft .NET, and it will remove the issue of HTML web page dependencies by extending HTML elements.

The functionality for web components is already being adopted by several technologies. Corporations researching support for web components include Apple and Microsoft, while Mozilla, Google Chrome 36 and Opera will already support it. Google has also released the concept of Polymer that provides an overview for web components.

The enterprise technologies to watch in 2014.

There are several emerging technologies that corporations should adopt in order to avoid disruption in the market place. Technologies that allow businesses to align in the modern networked and open society will help companies stay relevant and meet the expectations of their customers.

Emerging technologies that corporations should watch for in 2014 include: 3D printers that allow corporations to focus on design rather than manufacturing, the Internet of Things (IoT) where all devices connect in a network, mobile business applications revolutionizing remote support and services, noSQL and relational databases where products like Hadoop bring enterprise insight through big data, on-demand everything with delivery of any cloud service, Bring-Your-Own-Technology (BYOT) causing competition for tech acquisition, gamification to change approach to business solutions, wearable IT that leads to the quantified enterprise which will provide busines insights, virtualization. Among others include: cloud computing, integrated customer experiences, digital learning and MOOCs, open APIs, business social, global solutions networks, and a collaborative economy.

Mashups: The new breed of Web app.

Mashups are composed of content from unrelated data sources for people, and are often platform agnostic. They often aggregate third-party data and often provide some interactivity across the web. An example of a mashup is the ChicagoCrome.org site that integrates Google Maps and the Chicago Police Department database.

The mashup architecture is often made of three components: API/content providers, the mashup site, and the client's Web browser. There are also distinct genres of mashups, including mapping, video and photo, search and shopping, and news mashups. Other technologies used often include AJAX, SOAP and REST, screen scraping, semantic web, and RSS and ATOM. However, this architecture often brings up technical challenges such as data integration for semantic meaning and using components. There are many social issues around the idea of mashups due to problems with intellectual property.
